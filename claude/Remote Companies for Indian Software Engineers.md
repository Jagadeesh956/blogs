## 🦊 GitLab

### About
GitLab is arguably the world's most famous all-remote company — it has **no physical headquarters** and employs people across **65+ countries**. They build an AI-powered DevSecOps platform used by millions of developers. Their entire company handbook is publicly available online and is the gold standard for remote work culture.

### 🛠️ Required Skills (Recent Postings)
- **Backend**: Ruby on Rails, Go, PostgreSQL, Redis
- **Frontend**: Vue.js, GraphQL, TypeScript
- **DevOps/Infra**: Kubernetes, Terraform, GCP/AWS, CI/CD pipelines
- **Security**: AppSec, pen testing, SAST/DAST
- **General**: Strong async written communication, Git, open-source contribution history valued

### 💰 Compensation (India)
- Software Engineer (Mid): ₹25L–₹45L/yr
- Senior Engineer: ₹45L–₹80L/yr
- Staff/Principal: ₹80L–₹1.5Cr/yr
- Includes: equity (RSUs), home office stipend, professional development budget, 25 days PTO

### 🎯 Interview Difficulty: 🔴 Hard
GitLab interviews are thorough but transparent — they publish the entire process in their handbook.

**Hiring Process:**
1. **Application** → Resume + cover letter via careers.gitlab.com
2. **Recruiter Screen** (30 min, video) — culture & background check
3. **Technical Interview** (60–90 min) — live coding, system design, code review
4. **Hiring Manager Interview** — role-specific discussion, async work philosophy
5. **Team Interview** (Zoom) — meet potential teammates
6. **Reference checks** → Offer

**What they look for:** Strong async communication, ability to work independently, passion for open source, solid documentation habits.

### 🔗 How to Apply
- **Careers page**: https://about.gitlab.com/jobs/
- **Tips**: Contribute to GitLab's open-source repo before applying. Read the handbook thoroughly. Tailor your cover letter to their values (transparency, iteration, collaboration).

## 🔵 Automattic

### About
Automattic is behind WordPress.com, WooCommerce, Tumblr, Jetpack, and more. With **2,000+ employees across 96 countries**, they've been fully distributed since 2005. They are a pioneer of remote work with a strong writing-first culture. Salaries are location-independent — everyone earns the same regardless of where they live.

- **Mobile**: iOS (Swift), Android (Kotlin), React Native
- **Data**: SQL, Python, data pipelines, A/B testing
- **General**: Strong writing skills, async-first mindset, self-direction

### 💰 Compensation (India)

- Senior Engineer: ₹40L–₹65L/yr

## 🔑 General Tips for Landing Remote Jobs as an Indian Engineer

### 🧰 Skills That Help Across All Remote Companies
- **Async communication**: Excellent written English — emails, Slack, Notion docs
- **Documentation**: Ability to write clear technical specs, READMEs, and RFCs
- **Time zone flexibility**: Many US companies need 4–6 hr overlap with PST/EST
- **Self-management**: Demonstrating you can deliver without micromanagement
- **Open-source contributions**: GitHub activity is heavily weighted

### 📋 Resume & Profile Checklist
- [ ] Strong GitHub profile with pinned, documented projects
- [ ] LinkedIn fully optimized (open to work, remote filter on)
- [ ] Portfolio website or personal blog showing technical depth
- [ ] Contributions to open-source projects (even small ones count)
- [ ] LeetCode/HackerRank profile (Medium+ problems solved)
- [ ] Strong cover letters — many remote companies weigh these heavily
- [ ] Remote work experience highlighted prominently

### 🌐 Best Job Boards for Remote Roles
- **We Work Remotely** — weworkremotely.com (top remote-first board)
- **Remote OK** — remoteok.com
- **Himalayas** — himalayas.app
- **Arc.dev** — arc.dev (curated remote engineering roles)
- **Working Nomads** — workingnomads.com
- **Remote.co** — remote.co
- **LinkedIn** — filter: Remote + Worldwide
- **AngelList/Wellfound** — wellfound.com (startups)
- **Y Combinator Jobs** — ycombinator.com/jobs

### 💡 Platform-Specific Strategy (for Talent Networks)
If applying to **Turing, Toptal, or Arc.dev**, treat the vetting as your main interview:
- Practice LeetCode **Medium to Hard** daily for 4–8 weeks before applying
- Revise system design (read *Designing Data-Intensive Applications*)
- Polish your spoken and written English — communication is judged from day one
- Have 2–3 projects demonstrating real-world impact (not just tutorial projects)

### ⚠️ Watch Out For
- **Time zone requirements**: Many roles need IST overlap with US hours (evenings in India)
- **Payment**: Ensure clarity on INR vs USD payment, tax implications, and freelance vs full-time contracts
- **EOR vs direct hire**: Some companies use Employers of Record (like Deel, Remote.com) to hire you legally in India — this is normal and safe
- **Scams**: Legitimate remote jobs never ask for upfront fees or equipment deposits
